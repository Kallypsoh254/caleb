Common Questions

What Clients Usually Ask

What's your typical engagement model?

I work on both contract and full-time basis depending on the project scope. For consulting engagements, I prefer 3-6 month commitments to ensure meaningful impact. I'm flexible with remote work but available for on-site visits when necessary.

What industries have you worked in?

My experience spans fintech, e-commerce, SaaS platforms, IoT, and healthcare tech. I've built payment processing systems, inventory management platforms, real-time analytics dashboards, and compliance-focused applications. This diverse background helps me bring best practices from different domains.

How do you approach legacy system migrations?

I use the strangler fig pattern—gradually replacing old components while maintaining system stability. This involves building new services alongside legacy code, implementing feature flags, comprehensive testing, and zero-downtime deployment strategies. The goal is continuous delivery without disrupting operations.

Do you work with startups or enterprises?

Both! Startups benefit from my ability to build MVPs quickly and establish solid technical foundations for scaling. Enterprises value my experience with complex migrations, system optimization, and mentoring engineering teams. Each has unique challenges that I find equally rewarding.

What's your communication style during projects?

I believe in transparent, frequent communication. Weekly progress reports, bi-weekly demos, and daily async updates via Slack or similar tools. I document decisions in ADRs (Architecture Decision Records) and maintain updated technical documentation. You'll always know the project status, blockers, and next steps.

How do you handle scope changes?

Agile methodology embraces change. We prioritize features in the backlog, and scope adjustments are discussed during sprint planning. I provide impact assessments for changes—how they affect timeline, budget, and technical complexity. The goal is flexibility while maintaining project momentum and quality.
